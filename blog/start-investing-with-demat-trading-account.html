In today’s digital era, investing has become smarter and faster. It all begins with opening a Demat account. A Demat (Dematerialised) account acts as a digital vault that securely holds your shares, mutual funds, and other securities in electronic form. It eliminates the need for paper statements / certificates and makes buying, selling, and transferring securities simple and efficient through platforms like our iInvest App.

Your Demat account like a digital account just as your bank account holds money; your Demat account holds securities.

The main difference between holding shares physically and in Demat form is convenience and safety: physical certificates can get lost or damaged, while Demat holdings remain safe, easy to transfer, and instantly tradable.

It’s mandatory in India to have a Demat account if you wish to invest in stocks, ETFs, or corporate bonds etc. as per SEBI regulations.

As per SEBI regulations, any investor, including minors, through guardians and joint holders, is eligible to open a Demat account. You can also nominate a family member to ensure smooth transfer of assets in the future.
