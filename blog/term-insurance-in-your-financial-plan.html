Why Term Insurance is a Must-Have in Your Financial Plan

Insurance Protection Planning
Share now

Savings, investments, and retirement planning are usually the first things that come to mind when creating a strong financial base. But one of the least talked-about, perhaps the most ignored yet vital components, is term insurance. While investing is necessary, it should be secondary to the protection of your family from the uncertainties of life. Term insurance is meant just for this purpose when the family needs it the most. At Integrated, we feel that a good financial plan has to be both growth and protection.

Term Insurance: An Umbrella for the Family

Term insurance is a method by which high coverages are offered for comparatively low premiums. Nominees of the insured person get a lump sum payout if death occurs within the term of the insurance policy. This amount could be used by the ones left behind to maintain their standard of living, service any debts, cover educational costs, or finally take care of any day-to-day expenses.

Though most individuals buy health insurance to protect themselves against medical expenses, term insurance guarantees financial protection for the family in the total loss of income. Both complement each other, offering maximum protection.

Cover What Matters Most

Family health insurance might help in covering hospital charges, but it does not compensate for the loss of income. Term insurance does this very thing: making sure your dependents do not have to downshift their lifestyle. This is especially important if you are the sole breadwinner.

Conclusion

In today's world full of uncertainty, term insurance ensures peace of mind and economic security for a long period.
