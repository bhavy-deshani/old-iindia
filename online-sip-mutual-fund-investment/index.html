FAQ For Mutual Fund SIP

- A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) is a mutual fund investment strategy that involves investing a fixed amount of money at regular intervals. SIPs are similar to recurring deposits, where a small amount is automatically debited from a bank account and invested in a mutual fund on a set schedule. SIPs can be set up on a weekly, monthly, quarterly, semi-annual, or annual basis, and the investment amount can be as low as ₹500 per month.

SIPs are a popular way to invest in mutual funds because they can help investors build wealth, manage risk, and achieve financial goals like retirement planning.

SIPs work on two principles:

Rupee cost averaging

Regular investing can help investors avoid market volatility by buying more units when the market is down and fewer units when the market is up.

Compounding

Saving a small amount of money regularly can have a big impact on an investment over time.

- A SIP is generally considered a safe way to invest in mutual funds. This is because they spread the risk over the entire investment tenure instead of concentrating it at the beginning of the investment period.

If your primary investment goal is capital preservation and you do not expect higher returns from it, you can invest in an FD. Also if you have a fixed investment term in mind, you can invest in a fixed deposit scheme.

If you want to make goal-oriented investments which would fetch you higher returns, invest in a SIP.

Any investor persisting with SIPs during a falling market will regain the lost value before the market itself.

You may be familiar with the magic of compounding with SIPs.

As the invested amount grows with time and returns are reinvested, it can lead to higher returns in the future.
